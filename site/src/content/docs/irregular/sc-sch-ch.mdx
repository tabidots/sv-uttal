*sc*, *sch*, and *ch* are foreign letter combinations with no defined pronunciation in Swedish, while *stg* is a rare combination with an optional exceptional pronunciation.

## *sc* (2)

The SAOL (Svenska Akademiens ordlista) lists these words as being pronounced with a tj-sound, while Braxen lists them as sj-sound. Depending on your device's text-to-speech engine, you may hear one or the other when you play the audio.

- **la<Mark>sc</Mark>ivitet** <AudioButton word="lascivitet" />
- **o<Mark>sc</Mark>illation** <AudioButton word="oscillation" /> (3): o<Mark>sc</Mark>illation, o<Mark>sc</Mark>illering, o<Mark>sc</Mark>illograf

## *stg* (2)

These can optionally be pronounced as written (e.g., *vast•jöte*).

## *sch*

Typically pronounced "rs" at the end of a syllable, to approximate the German "sch". The exceptions seem to occur at the beginning of a syllable.
