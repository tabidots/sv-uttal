This analysis is the result of examining approximately 5000 distinct adjective lexemes in Wiktionary using pronunciation data from the Braxen dataset.

<Aside type="note">
This analysis only covers regular adjectives. Since irregular adjectives have forms that are derived from completely different words (*lite > mindre*) or undergo a stem change (*lﾃ･ng > lﾃ､ngre*), and there are very few such adjectives, there is no real need to cover them here.
</Aside>

## Polysyllabic adjectives: acute to grave

Only 19 adjectives shift from acute to grave between their base form and the other forms. Interestingly, they all end in unstressed *-en, -el, -er*, just like the polysyllabic nouns that shift from acute to grave between singular and plural (*ﾃｶken, titel, fjﾃ､der...*).

All other unprefixed adjectives (916) have a stable pitch accent pattern, whether that be grave or acute.

## Prefixed adjectives

The same principles as verbs apply here. All prefixes take away any secondary stress in the root word, and additionally, prefixes other than *be-* and *fﾃｶr-* "steal" that stress. This results in a stable accent pattern across all forms.
